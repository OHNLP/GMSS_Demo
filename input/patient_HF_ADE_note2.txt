ASSESSMENT/PLAN:

1. Heart Failure with Preserved Ejection Fraction (HFpEF)
Patient with known HFpEF now presenting with weight gain and increased lower extremity edema. BNP mildly elevated at 310. Volume status mildly overloaded. Will continue hydrochlorothiazide and empagliflozin (which also provides diuretic and cardiac benefit in HFpEF). Dietary sodium restriction reinforced. Follow-up echo in 3 months.

2. Dry cough – suspected ADE from lisinopril
Patient developed a persistent dry cough approximately 2 weeks after starting lisinopril, a classic adverse effect of ACE inhibitor therapy. Given the significant impact on quality of life and sleep, will discontinue lisinopril and transition to losartan 50 mg daily (ARB), which does not carry the same cough side effect. Counsel patient that cough should resolve within 1–4 weeks.

3. Bilateral ankle edema – suspected ADE from amlodipine
Patient's ankle swelling is temporally consistent with initiation of amlodipine 10 mg daily, a well-known adverse effect of dihydropyridine calcium channel blockers due to peripheral vasodilation. Will reduce amlodipine to 5 mg daily. Blood pressure to be monitored closely given additional dose reduction. If edema persists, may consider switching to alternative antihypertensive agent.

4. New-onset atrial fibrillation with rapid ventricular response
Holter confirms paroxysmal AFib. In the context of HFpEF, rate control preferred. Will initiate metoprolol succinate 25 mg daily for rate control. Given CHADS2-VASc score of 4 (female sex, age ≥65, hypertension, diabetes), anticoagulation indicated. Will start apixaban 5 mg twice daily. Referred to electrophysiology for further evaluation. Patient counseled on stroke risk and signs of bleeding.

5. Hypertension – suboptimally controlled
BP 148/92 today despite lisinopril (to be discontinued) and amlodipine (being reduced). Transitioning to losartan as above. Will reassess BP in 2 weeks and uptitrate losartan if needed. Continue lifestyle modifications: low-sodium diet, weight reduction.
